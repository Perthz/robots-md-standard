Proposed Open Standard

robots.md

A discovery standard for the age of AI agents

Read the Spec ↓ Why This Matters
01 / The Problem

The web was built for humans and search engines. AI agents need something new.

robots.txt has served us well since 1994. It tells crawlers where they can and can't go. But it was designed for a world where the consumer of web content was either a human reading a page or a search engine indexing links.

We're now entering a world where AI agents act on behalf of humans — booking appointments, comparing services, making purchases, integrating systems. These agents don't need HTML layouts or meta descriptions. They need structured, machine-readable content that describes what a business does, what APIs are available, and how to interact programmatically.

There is currently no standard for this. Every AI agent that wants to understand a business has to scrape, guess, or rely on incomplete search results. We can do better.

robots.txt (1994)

  • Controls crawler access permissions
  • Designed for search engine indexing
  • Says where bots CAN'T go
  • No business context or content
  • No API discovery

robots.md (2026)

  • Describes business for AI agents
  • Designed for AI agent interaction
  • Says what agents CAN do
  • Rich business context and metadata
  • Full API endpoint discovery
02 / Specification v0.1

The robots.md standard

A robots.md file is a Markdown document placed at the root of a website (/robots.md) that provides AI agents with structured, human-readable information about a business and its available digital interfaces.

Filename
robots.md
Location
Website root: https://example.com/robots.md
Format
Markdown with structured sections (GitHub-flavoured)
Encoding
UTF-8
Content-Type
text/markdown; charset=UTF-8
Max Size
Recommended under 50KB
Relation to robots.txt
Complementary, not a replacement

Required Sections

A valid robots.md must include at minimum:

robots.md — minimum viable file 
# Business Name

## Identity
- Organization: Your Business Name
- Website: https://example.com
- Industry: What you do
- Location: Where you operate
- Contact: hello@example.com
- Updated: 2026-03-10

## Services
A description of what the business offers,
written for an AI agent to understand context.

## Endpoints (optional)
| Endpoint | Method | Auth | Description |
|----------|--------|------|-------------|
| /api/info | GET | None | Business info |

## Data & Privacy
How data is handled, stored, and protected.

## Permissions
- Cache: yes (refresh: 24h)
- Attribution: Required
- Commercial use: Contact for licensing

Optional Sections

Businesses can extend their robots.md with additional structured data:

Extended sections 
## Pricing
Structured pricing information for AI agents
to compare or recommend services.

## Case Studies
Results and outcomes that help AI agents
evaluate and recommend the business.

## Integration Guide
How AI agents should interact with APIs,
including rate limits, auth methods, and examples.

## FAQ
Structured Q&A for AI agents to answer
user queries about the business.

## Changelog
Version history of the robots.md file.

Discovery Mechanism

For AI agents to find robots.md automatically, websites should implement one or more of these discovery methods:

Method 1: HTML <link> tag 
<link rel="ai-discovery" href="/robots.md" type="text/markdown" />
Method 2: robots.txt reference 
# robots.txt
User-agent: *
Allow: /

# AI Agent Discovery
AI-discovery: /robots.md
Method 3: .well-known endpoint 
// GET /.well-known/ai-discovery
{
  "robots_md": "/robots.md",
  "content_api": "/api/content",
  "version": "0.1"
}
Method 4: HTTP response header 
X-AI-Discovery: /robots.md
03 / Use Cases

What becomes possible

When AI agents can reliably discover structured business information, entirely new interactions become possible:

For Businesses

Control how AI agents describe your services. Provide accurate pricing, capabilities, and contact info directly — instead of letting AI hallucinate about your business based on scraped fragments.

For AI Agent Developers

Build agents that can discover, compare, and interact with businesses programmatically. No more scraping unreliable HTML. One standard location, one structured format.

For Consumers

Ask your AI assistant to "find a plumber in Auckland who does after-hours work" and get accurate, up-to-date results pulled directly from the business — not from a stale directory listing.

For the Ecosystem

As AI agents increasingly mediate commerce (see Mastercard Agent Pay, OpenAI Operator, etc.), businesses without machine-readable presence will become invisible. robots.md is the simplest path to AI-readiness.

04 / Design Principles

Built on simplicity

Human-readable first. Markdown was chosen because it's readable by both humans and machines. No XML schemas, no JSON-LD complexity. A business owner should be able to write their own robots.md in a text editor.

Progressive complexity. The minimum viable file is 10 lines. Advanced features (API endpoints, integration guides, structured pricing) are optional. Start simple, add depth as needed.

Complementary, not competing. robots.md doesn't replace robots.txt, sitemap.xml, or schema.org markup. It fills a gap none of them address: telling AI agents what a business IS and how to interact with it.

Privacy by design. The standard includes a Data & Privacy section as required, not optional. Businesses declare upfront how data is handled, cached, and attributed.
